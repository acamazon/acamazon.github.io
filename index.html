About me

I am a PhD. student in Astronomy at the University of Florida. I am working with Dr. Rafael Guzman on extragalactic astronomy and observational cosmology. We are using the Gran Telescopio de Canarias (GTC) to study HII galaxies at low and high redshift with MEGARA and EMIR instruments. We are also working on the Ultra Low Surface Brightness Universe, specially, on the ESA F-class mission, ARRAKIHS, which will study the nature of the dark matter.

Biography

I grew up in Valladolid, Spain, where I studied three of the four years of my Physics degree. In 2019 I obtained a SICUE mobility grant studentship, from Universidad de Valladolid to Universidad Complutense de Madrid, where I finished my B.Sc. In 2020 and I studied my M.Sc. in Astrophysics at Universidad Complutense de Madrid. I also work at the Centro Astron贸mico de Tiedra, Spain, as a collaborative astronomer since 2018 where I have the opportunity to bring astronomy closer to the general public and do outreach. I have also done different internships that have helped me to develop my skills and define my interests in science. In 2019 I did an internship at the Atmospheric Optics Group at University of Valladolid (GOA-UVa). And in 2021 I did another internship at SATLANTIS Micro-satellites at Bilbao, Spain. I arrived to the University of Florida in 2022 looking forward to obtaining a PhD. in Astronomy focused on the observational cosmology.
